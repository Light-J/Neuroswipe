The somatosensory homunculus
The sensory strip contains a map of our body. This is called the somatosensory homunculus and is shown below. When you touch something soft with your hand, this information will travel up to the sensory strip to where the hand is mapped on the homunculus.

Neurosurgeons use the homunculus as anatomical guidance during surgery. For example, if they touch the wrist mapped on the sensory strip, the patient may feel as if someone is actually touching their wrist!
